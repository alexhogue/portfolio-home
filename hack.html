Hack@Brown

Webpage and branding for a student organization

Overview

Hack@Brown is Brown University's annual hackathon for high school and college students from around the country. Each year, we have a different playful theme, to encourage students of all backgrounds to particupate, which requires a new website and branding.

The theme this year was "Outer Space", so our website and branding is all inspired by this concept.

My Role

As a design lead, I handled art direction, and I worked to delegate team responsibilities, schedule and lead team meetings, and make final design decisions on social media, web design and branding.

Project Focus:

Web design, brand identity

Design Team:

Vivian Li (co-lead) and a wonderful design team

Tools:

Figma, Blender

Problem

How can we create a culture of inclusivity to encourage even those who never coded before to participate in Hack@Brown?

Solution

The hackathon theme "Space" emphasizes exploration and encourages anyone with a willingness to learn to participate. 3D visuals also immerse the participant in a fun and "hands-on" startup environment.

Design Choices

Based on our initial sketches, as a group, we gathered the elements we liked most. We then developed a wireframe mockup in Figma and began developing our 3D assets in Blender.

Style Guide

Taking inspiration from our mood board, we wanted to create a futuristic and vibrant style using a unique font for headings and vibrant accent colors.

High-fi Prototype

High-fi for desktop:

  • We aimed to capture outer space at the top descending down to earth as you scroll to the bottom using a gradient from a dark to a light blue and the earth asset at the footer.
  • Animation was used to make the webpage visually eyecatching and unique

High-fi for mobile:

  • The mobile version has fewer assets compared to the desktop version in order to reduce business on the smaller screen.
    • This makes the screen clearer and more accessible to mobile users, which would likely be a primary use during the hackathon when participants are busy and on the go.

Key Takeaways

  • Leading a team is very different from simply being on a team and requires solid decision making and committment but is very rewarding in the end.
  • When working to make a website engaging and accessible for a wide audience, it is important to be creative in design decisions!
  • Vibrant colors and simplified 3D illustrations can create an immersive website homepage that appeals to users of all backgrounds and experience levels.
